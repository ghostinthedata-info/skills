# Presentation Planner

A fill-in worksheet for planning an explanatory data communication, plus a
pre-send checklist. Do this **before** opening any slideware or charting tool.
Based on *Storytelling with Data* (Knaflic).

---

## 1. Who / What / How

Answer in order. Don't touch the data until Who and What are locked.

```markdown
## Who (the audience)
- Specific audience / decision maker: <name a person or tight group — not "stakeholders">
- My relationship & credibility with them: <established expert / first contact / need to earn trust>
- Their biases / what makes them resistant or supportive: <...>

## What (the action)
- What I need them to KNOW: <one line>
- What I need them to DO (explicit ask): <approve / fund / change / adopt / stop …>
- What a successful outcome looks like: <...>

## How (the evidence)
- Data that supports the point: <...>
- Opposing/contextual data I must not hide: <...>

## Tone
- <celebratory | urgent | cautionary | neutral>

## Mechanism (pick one primary vehicle)
- [ ] Live presentation  → sparse slides, detail held in head
- [ ] Written / emailed document → carries detail, pre-empts questions
- ⚠️ Don't build one "slideument" to do both.
```

---

## 2. 3-minute story & Big Idea

```markdown
## 3-minute story (the elevator version)
<A short paragraph you could say with no slides if your slot were cut to 3 min.>

## Big Idea (one sentence — must pass all three)
- [ ] States my unique point of view
- [ ] Conveys what's at stake
- [ ] Is a complete sentence
> Big Idea: <one sentence>
```

Lead the communication with the Big Idea.

---

## 3. Storyboard (low-tech)

Sketch the narrative flow on paper / whiteboard / Post-its — one idea per note
so you can reorder. Get stakeholder sign-off on this before building.

```markdown
1. <opening / Big Idea>
2. <context the audience needs>
3. <the key evidence — the pearls>
4. <the recommendation / the ask>
```

---

## 4. Per-visual choice

For each chart, justify the choice or change it.

```markdown
- Message of this visual: <the single takeaway>
- Visual type: <text | table | line | slopegraph | bar | heatmap>
- Why not a simpler one (big number / text)? <...>
- Title states the takeaway (not the axes)? <e.g. "Support volume fell 30%">
```

---

## 5. Pre-send checklist

```markdown
- [ ] Explanatory, not exploratory — every slice earns its place; oysters cut.
- [ ] There is an explicit ask for action (not just "any questions?").
- [ ] Audience is specific; message fits *them*.
- [ ] Big Idea leads and is one complete sentence.
- [ ] Right visual for each message; no pie/donut, no 3D, no secondary axis.
- [ ] Bars baselined at zero.
- [ ] Decluttered: borders/gridlines/redundant legends removed (data-ink).
- [ ] Attention directed: one accent colour, context greyed; works in greyscale
      and for colour-blind viewers.
- [ ] Every chart title carries the takeaway; labels direct, not detached.
```
