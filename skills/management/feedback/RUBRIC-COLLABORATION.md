# Rubric — Collaboration & Communication

Rate against the **Meet** bar for the engineer's *expected* level. Underperform = below it; Meet = at it; Exceed = at/above the next level's Meet bar.

---

## 1. Written & verbal clarity
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | Messages are unclear; others have to decode. | Communicates status and questions clearly; asks well-formed questions. | Writes crisply for the audience. |
| Mid | Explanations assume too much; buries the point. | Explains technical work clearly to peers; writes readable design notes/PR descriptions. | Tailors message to audience; makes complex things simple. |
| Senior | Loses non-expert audiences; over- or under-explains. | Communicates designs and tradeoffs clearly to mixed audiences incl. non-technical stakeholders. | Writing influences decisions; widely referenced. |
| Staff | — | Communicates strategy persuasively across teams and to leadership. | Shapes org narrative on data. |
| Principal | — | Articulates vision compellingly org/industry-wide. | — |

## 2. Stakeholder management
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | Avoids stakeholders; mis-sets expectations. | Interacts professionally; relays needs and constraints accurately. | Builds trust with immediate stakeholders. |
| Mid | Over-promises or goes silent under pressure. | Manages expectations for owned work; says no/renegotiates appropriately. | Turns stakeholders into collaborators. |
| Senior | Stakeholder friction or misalignment on owned systems. | Manages competing stakeholder needs for a system; negotiates scope and priority. | Builds durable cross-functional trust. |
| Staff | — | Aligns stakeholders across teams; brokers competing priorities. | Trusted advisor to senior leadership. |
| Principal | — | Shapes stakeholder strategy at exec level. | — |

## 3. Code review (giving & receiving)
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | Defensive about feedback; reviews superficially. | Acts on review feedback graciously; gives basic, kind reviews. | Reviews thoughtfully beyond style nits. |
| Mid | Reviews are nitpicky or rubber-stamps; misses substance. | Gives substantive, kind, timely reviews; receives feedback as a gift. | Uses review to teach, not just gate. |
| Senior | Review is a bottleneck or a blunt instrument. | Sets review tone/standards; balances rigour with velocity; mentors via review. | Makes review a culture-building force. |
| Staff | — | Drives review/quality culture across teams. | Org engineering-culture influence. |
| Principal | — | Sets the bar for technical rigour org-wide. | — |

## 4. Knowledge sharing
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | Hoards or hides what they learn. | Shares learnings with the team; asks and answers openly. | Volunteers to write things up / demo. |
| Mid | Knowledge stays siloed in their head. | Actively shares context; onboards/helps peers. | Creates lasting shared resources. |
| Senior | Becomes a single point of knowledge failure. | Spreads expertise deliberately; reduces bus-factor on owned systems. | Builds team learning mechanisms. |
| Staff | — | Drives knowledge-sharing practices across teams. | Org learning culture. |
| Principal | — | Develops the org's technical communication norms. | — |
