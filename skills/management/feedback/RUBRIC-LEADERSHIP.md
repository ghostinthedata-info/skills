# Rubric — Leadership & Influence

Rate against the **Meet** bar for the engineer's *expected* level. Underperform = below it; Meet = at it; Exceed = at/above the next level's Meet bar.

Note: at Junior/Mid the *expectation* is emerging influence, not formal leadership. Don't penalise a junior for "not leading" — rate against their level's Meet bar, which is modest here.

---

## 1. Mentoring & growing others
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | Unwilling to help peers; competitive. | Helps peers when asked; pairs willingly. | Helps newer joiners get unstuck unprompted. |
| Mid | Help is sporadic or unclear. | Mentors juniors on specific skills; gives useful guidance. | Actively grows a junior's capability over time. |
| Senior | Hoards interesting work; team doesn't grow around them. | Multiplies the team — mentors, delegates growth-stretch work, builds others up. | Visibly levels-up multiple engineers. |
| Staff | — | Develops senior engineers; scales mentoring across teams. | Builds the org's technical talent pipeline. |
| Principal | — | Grows the org's senior+ technical leaders. | — |

## 2. Technical direction & decision-making
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | Waits to be told everything; no opinions. | Forms reasoned opinions on own work; makes sound small decisions. | Brings well-argued options to the table. |
| Mid | Decisions are ad hoc; can't justify tradeoffs. | Makes sound technical decisions for a feature; explains the tradeoffs. | Makes good calls in ambiguity without escalating unnecessarily. |
| Senior | Indecisive in ambiguity, or decides without buy-in. | Sets local technical direction; makes defensible calls under uncertainty; documents the why. | Direction is trusted and adopted beyond their team. |
| Staff | — | Drives technical strategy across teams; builds consensus around hard calls. | Org technical direction. |
| Principal | — | Sets multi-year technical strategy; makes the bet-the-roadmap calls. | — |

## 3. Driving alignment & influence
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | (Not expected.) | Voices concerns/ideas in team settings. | Influences small team decisions with good arguments. |
| Mid | Goes along silently or argues unproductively. | Advocates effectively for sound choices; disagrees-and-commits well. | Brings the team along on a direction. |
| Senior | Can't move people without authority; creates friction. | Influences without authority; builds consensus on contested technical calls. | Aligns multiple stakeholders behind a direction. |
| Staff | — | Drives alignment across teams on cross-cutting bets. | Moves the org on strategic direction. |
| Principal | — | Aligns leadership behind org/function-level strategy. | — |

## 4. Raising the bar / ownership of standards
| Level | Underperform | **Meet** | Exceed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior | Indifferent to quality norms. | Follows and respects team standards. | Suggests small improvements to how the team works. |
| Mid | Cuts corners when convenient. | Upholds standards consistently; improves own practices. | Raises a specific team practice measurably. |
| Senior | Tolerates erosion of quality on owned systems. | Sets and defends standards for a team/system; addresses tech debt deliberately. | Institutes practices the team adopts as default. |
| Staff | — | Raises engineering standards across teams. | Defines org engineering excellence. |
| Principal | — | Sets the org's technical bar and culture. | — |
